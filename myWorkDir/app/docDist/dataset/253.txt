The ''RMS Titanic'' left Southampton, England on April 10, 1912 for New York City. The ship sank on April 14, resulting in the death of 1,500 people.<ref name=":8">Tikkanen, A. (October 13, 2019). Encyclopaedia Britannica Inc. <nowiki>https://www.britannica.com/topic/Titanic</nowiki></ref> This infamous disaster and enormous loss of life was the result of multiple poor ethical decisions that were made months before the ship set sail during its construction, in the hours leading up to the collision, and during the sinking itself. 
== Ship Design ==
The [[wikipedia:RMS Titanic|''Titanic'']] was built for the [[wikipedia:White Star Line|White Star Line]] by [[wikipedia:Harland and Wolff|Harland and Wolff]] of Belfast.<ref>National Archives. (2012, March). Titanic. <nowiki>https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/titanic/</nowiki></ref> Weighing close to 47,000 tons, she cost about 1.5 billion pounds to build and had 8 triple expansion engines, with steam supplied by 159 furnaces. The hull was divided into 16 supposedly watertight compartments. The amenities on board the ''Titanic'' were extravagant and included a swimming pool, three electric elevators, a gymnasium, and a Turkish bath.<ref name=":0">Cronan, James. (2011, January).
