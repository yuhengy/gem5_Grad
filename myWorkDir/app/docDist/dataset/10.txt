===COVID-19===
The game industry booms, as people hunker down at home during the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Browne |first1=Ryan |title=Game consoles may haul in $45 billion this year, and big changes COVID-19.<ref>{{cite web |last1=McWhertor |first1=Michael |title=No More Heroes 3 delayed to 2021 |url=https://www.polygon.com/2020/9/10/21430485/no-more-heroes-3-delayed-release-date-nintendo-switch Game development companies move to work from home, with some such as Square Enix planning to allow work from home after the pandemic ends.<ref>{{cite web |last1=G |first1=Alec |title=Nintendo May 2020}}</ref> 

The pandemic simulation game ''Plague Inc.'', which was originally released eight years prior in 2012,<ref>{{cite news |title=How to Get Rich Simulating the Deaths of Billions of People |url=https://www.wired.com/2012/12/plague-inc/ |access-date=21 January 2021 |work=Wired |language=en-us}}</ref> gains massive popularity, with the developers releasing an update to enhance the educational experience of the game in light of the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="Nature Inc">{{cite journal |last1=Thompson |first1=Benjamin |last2=Howe |first2=Nick |title=Could you prevent a pandemic? A very 2020 video game |journal=Nature |date=16 December 2020 |doi=10.1038/d41586-020-03594-6 |url=https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-020-03594-6#MO0 |access-date=21 January 2021 |language=en}}</ref> The developer of Plague Inc. also donated $250,000 to CEPI and WHO to help fight the pandemic.<ref name="Nature Inc"/> The older 2008 flash game ''Pandemic 2'' also experiences a surge of popularity.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Leskin |first1=Paige |title=An old flash computer game is getting a 2nd life because of its eerie similarities to the coronavirus outbreak, and its website's CEO says it has too much of an 'educational value' to shut it down |url=https://www.businessinsider.com/coronavirus-pandemic-2-computer-game-second-life-amid-outbreak-2020-3 |access-date=27 January 2021 |work=Business Insider}}</ref>

In 2021 the ''Truck Simulator'' series held a virtual trucking event in solidarity with real truckers delivering COVID-19 vaccines.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Good |first1=Owen S. |title=Truck Simulator event invites players to deliver COVID-19 vaccines |url=https://www.polygon.com/2021/1/22/22244780/euro-truck-simulator-2-american-hauling-hope-covid-event-dates |access-date=23 January 2021 |work=Polygon |date=22 January 2021 |language=en}}</ref> Players from around the globe organized a virtual graduation ceremony using ''Minecraft''.<ref>{{cite news |last2=Anderson |first2=Pearse |title=Inside a Global Graduation Ceremony, Held Entirely in 'Minecraft' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/global-graduation-quaranteen-minecraft-1007713/ |access-date=29 January 2021 |work=Rolling Stone |date=1 June 2020}}</ref>

COVID-19 increased the public profile of virtual reality applications,<ref>{{cite news |title=Coronavirus: Is virtual reality tourism about to take off? |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-54658147 |access-date=1 January 2021 |work=BBC News |date=30 October 2020}}</ref> potentially increasing interest in VR gaming as a result. AR gaming proved to help some seeking reprieve from isolation.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ellis |first1=Louise A. |last2=Lee |first2=Matthew D. |last3=Ijaz |first3=Kiran |last4=Smith |first4=James |last5=Braithwaite |first5=Jeffrey |last6=Yin |first6=Kathleen |title=COVID-19 as 'Game Changer' for the Physical Activity and Mental Well-Being of Augmented Reality Game Players During the Pandemic: Mixed Methods Survey Study |url=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33284781/ |access-date=1 January 2021 |work=Journal of Medical Internet Research |date=22 December 2020 |pages=e25117 |doi=10.2196/25117}}</ref> As gyms closed, interest in Exergaming greatly increased by those looking to get fit while isolating.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Viana |first1=Ricardo Borges |last2=de Lira |first2=Claudio Andre Barbosa |title=Exergames as Coping Strategies for Anxiety Disorders During the COVID-19 Quarantine Period |journal=Games for Health Journal |date=June 2020 |volume=9 |issue=3 |pages=147–149 |doi=10.1089/g4h.2020.0060 |url=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32375011/ |access-date=1 January 2021 |issn=2161-7856}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Sarkar |first1=Samit |title=Ring Fit Adventure is sold out everywhere, Nintendo confirms |url=https://www.polygon.com/2020/3/13/21177214/ring-fit-adventure-sold-out-stock-coronavirus-nintendo-switch |access-date=1 January 2021 |work=Polygon |date=13 March 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

Though its launch was delayed by COVID-19, the first Super Mario World theme park in Osaka, Japan was shown in a video tour online by Mario creator Shigeru Miyamoto.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Peters |first1=Jay |title=Shigeru Miyamoto tours Super Nintendo World theme park in new video |url=https://www.theverge.com/2020/12/18/22189601/super-nintendo-world-theme-park-universal-studios-japan-mario-shigeru-miyamoto |access-date=19 December 2020 |work=The Verge |date=18 December 2020 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Machkovech |first1=Sam |title=Miyamoto leads fans through Super Nintendo World—and it looks incredible |url=https://arstechnica.com/gaming/2020/12/miyamoto-leads-fans-through-super-nintendo-world-and-it-looks-incredible/ |access-date=19 December 2020 |work=Ars Technica |date=19 December 2020 |language=en-us}}</ref>

===Apple Vs. Epic===
In August 2020, Apple and Epic begin disputing payment systems on the Apple App store, resulting in the game ''Fortnite'' being removed from the App Store.<ref>{{cite web |title='Fortnite' Developer Epic Games Is Suing Apple for Anti-Competitive Practices |url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/akzaa8/fortnite-developer-epic-games-is-suing-apple-for-anti-competitive-practices |website=www.vice.com |accessdate=20 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref> Coupled with increasing scrutiny from lawmakers, Apple reduces their cut from 30% to 15% for developers making under a million dollars annually, a move said to benefit indie game developers.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Leswing |first1=Kif |title=Apple will cut App Store commissions by half to 15% for small app makers |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/11/18/apple-will-cut-app-store-fees-by-half-to-15percent-for-small-developers.html |website=CNBC |access-date=2 December 2020 |language=en |date=18 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Axon |first1=Samuel |title=Apple drops its cut of App Store revenues from 30% to 15% for some developers |url=https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2020/11/apple-lowers-its-cut-of-app-store-revenues-for-some-developers/ |website=Ars Technica |access-date=2 December 2020 |language=en-us |date=18 November 2020}}</ref>
