==History==
===Development===
Development on the Xbox 360 started in 2003 as project Xenon.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Story Behind the Xbox |url=https://www.pcmag.com/news/the-story-behind-the-xbox |website=PCMAG |accessdate=14 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref> From 2003 to 2005 the dev kit for the Xbox 360 was based on an Apple PowerMac G5 desktop computer.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Mac That Helped Build The Xbox Rides Again |url=https://hackaday.com/2019/01/24/the-mac-that-helped-build-the-xbox-rides-again/ |website=Hackaday |access-date=7 December 2020 |date=24 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=The SDK “Power Mac G5” for the Xbox 360 |url=https://www.journaldulapin.com/2019/01/21/power-mac-g5-sdk/ |website=Le journal du lapin |access-date=7 December 2020 |language=fr |date=21 January 2019}}</ref>

The Xbox 360 exterior was designed by three different studios.<ref>{{cite web |last1=staff |first1=CNET Australia |title=Xbox 360 hardware specs in detail |url=https://www.cnet.com/news/xbox-360-hardware-specs-in-detail/ |website=CNET |accessdate=9 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

===Launch===
The Xbox 360 was launched on November 22nd, 2005.<ref>{{cite news |title=Microsoft Announces Xbox 360 Day One Launch Lineup — Strongest Launch in the History of Video Game Consoles |url=https://news.microsoft.com/2005/11/14/microsoft-announces-xbox-360-day-one-launch-lineup-strongest-launch-in-the-history-of-video-game-consoles/ |accessdate=28 October 2020 |work=Stories |date=14 November 2005}}</ref>

===Prime===

Early Xbox 360 consoles would often scratch disks or overheat to the point of bricking the console.<ref>{{cite web |title=Engineering Disaster: The Xbox 360 Red Ring of Death |url=https://www.designnews.com/engineering-disaster-xbox-360-red-ring-death |website=designnews.com |accessdate=13 November 2020 |language=en |date=26 October 2016}}</ref> In light of high failure rates, in 2007 Microsoft extended the Warranty of the Xbox 360 to three years, ultimately costing Microsoft $1.15 billion dollars.<ref name="Win Balmer"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Gilbert |first1=Ben |title=How Microsoft spent $1 billion on a simple mistake with its best game console |url=https://www.businessinsider.com/red-ring-of-death-recall-2016-4 |website=Business Insider |accessdate=13 November 2020}}</ref> The sheer scale of the recall was so massive that a significant portion of that cost, $240 million dollars, was solely dedicated to paying for the logistics of shipping consoles.<ref name="Win Balmer">{{cite news |title=Steve Ballmer saved the Xbox brand |url=https://www.windowscentral.com/peter-moore-xbox-one-wouldnt-have-happened-if-steve-ballmer-had-not-saved-xbox-360 |access-date=4 March 2021 |work=Windows Central |date=3 July 2015}}</ref> Legal action resulting from the high failure rates on the Xbox 360 would eventually lead to the US Supreme Court case ''Microsoft Corp. v. Baker''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Microsoft Corp. v. Baker |url=https://harvardlawreview.org/2017/11/microsoft-corp-v-baker/ |website=harvardlawreview.org |access-date=25 November 2020}}</ref>

The Xbox 360 S was released in 2010.<ref name="Xbox evolution">{{cite web |title=The Evolution Of Xbox Consoles |url=https://www.gamespot.com/gallery/the-evolution-of-xbox-consoles/2900-1322/#9 |website=GameSpot |accessdate=13 November 2020}}</ref> The price of Xbox Live also increased in 2010.<ref>{{cite news |title=Xbox Live Price Increase Coming November 1 |url=https://kotaku.com/xbox-live-price-increase-coming-november-1-5625516 |access-date=22 January 2021 |work=Kotaku |language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Carpenter |first1=Nicole |title=The price of Xbox Live Gold is going up |url=https://www.polygon.com/2021/1/22/22244203/xbox-live-gold-price-increase-subscription |access-date=22 January 2021 |work=Polygon |date=22 January 2021 |language=en}}</ref>

The Xbox 360 E was released in 2013.<ref name="Xbox evolution"/>

===Legacy===
Xbox 360 production ceased in 2016.<ref>{{cite news |title=Xbox 360 games console discontinued by Microsoft |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-36097461#:~:text=Microsoft%20has%20said%20it%20is,10%20years%20after%20it%20launched.&text=Mr%20Spencer%20said%20all%20remaining,be%20supported%20for%20existing%20users. |accessdate=28 October 2020 |work=BBC News |date=20 April 2016}}</ref> 85 million Xbox 360 consoles were sold.<ref>{{cite web |title=The bestselling consoles of all time |url=https://www.digitaltrends.com/gaming/bestselling-consoles-of-all-time/ |website=www.digitaltrends.com |accessdate=14 November 2020}}</ref>

==Technology==
===Compute===
The Xbox 360 is powered by a custom  three core IBM 64-bit PowerPC CPU named '''Xenon''', clocked at 3.2 gigahertz.<ref name="How Xbox360 works"/><ref name="Inside Xbox Xenon">{{cite web |last1=Stokes |first1=Jon |title=Inside the Xbox 360, Part II: the Xenon CPU |url=https://arstechnica.com/features/2005/06/xbox360-2/3/ |website=Ars Technica |accessdate=9 November 2020 |language=en-us |date=2 June 2005}}</ref> The CPU has 165 million transistors.<ref name="How Xbox360 works"/> The CPU also contains eFuses to prevent firmware rollbacks.<ref>{{cite web |title=eFuse blown, 360 homebrew now possible |url=https://www.engadget.com/2007-08-24-efuse-blown-360-homebrew-now-possible.html |website=Engadget |accessdate=9 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

The Xbox 360 GPU is a custom ATI design clocked at 500 megahertz, with 10 megabytes of dedicated RAM.<ref name="How Xbox360 works"/>

The Xbox 360 has 512 megabytes of unified GDDR3 RAM clocked at 700 megahertz.<ref name="Untapped Cell">{{cite web |title=The Untapped Potential of the PS3’s CELL Processor And How Naughty Dog Tamed The Beast |url=https://gamingbolt.com/the-untapped-potential-of-the-ps3s-cell-processor-and-how-naughty-dog-tamed-the-beast |accessdate=9 November 2020}}</ref><ref name="How Xbox360 works">{{cite web |title=How Xbox 360 Works |url=https://electronics.howstuffworks.com/xbox-three-sixty.htm |website=HowStuffWorks |accessdate=9 November 2020 |language=en |date=29 November 2006}}</ref> The Xbox 360 RAM is shared between the CPU and GPU.<ref name="Untapped Cell"/>

===Storage===

Most Xbox 360 units have removable hard drives.<ref>{{cite web |title=Xbox Support |url=https://support.xbox.com/en-US/help/xbox-360/accessories/add-remove-hard-drive |website=support.xbox.com |accessdate=13 November 2020}}</ref>

===Software===
Despite using a 64-bit CPU, ExtremeTech suggests that much of the code for the Xbox 360 was 32 bit.<ref>{{cite web |title=How Microsoft Built Xbox 360, Xbox Compatibility Into the Xbox One - ExtremeTech |url=https://www.extremetech.com/gaming/257851-microsoft-built-xbox-360-xbox-compatibility-xbox-one |website=www.extremetech.com |accessdate=9 November 2020}}</ref>
====Xbox Live====
In 2020 Xbox Live cloud saves were made available to free Xbox Live users on the Xbox 360.<ref>{{cite web |title=Xbox 360 Gets New Feature 15 Years Later |url=https://comicbook.com/gaming/news/xbox-360-cloud-saves-update-feature/ |website=GAMING |accessdate=13 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
